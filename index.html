AliExpress Shipping, Delivery & Returns

TL;DR – Standard shipping is reliable but slow; returns are possible if you follow the dispute and refund process. [jingsourcing](https://jingsourcing.com/b-how-to-buy-from-aliexpress/)

AliExpress offers multiple shipping options, ranging from free/low‑cost standard shipping to faster premium options. Standard shipping is often the cheapest choice but may take 15–30 days or more, depending on your country and customs checks. For non‑urgent items, this trade‑off between cost and speed is acceptable; for gifts or time‑sensitive purchases, you may want to spend more on quicker delivery. [yakkyofy](https://yakkyofy.com/how-does-aliexpress-work-heres-a-practical-guide/)

Once the seller ships your order, you receive a tracking number that you can use to monitor progress. The tracking page is usually accessible from your order details or via the shipping carrier’s website. If the tracking shows no updates after several days, or if the package never arrives, you can open a dispute and request a refund or partial compensation. [jingsourcing](https://jingsourcing.com/b-how-to-buy-from-aliexpress/)

What happens if my AliExpress order never arrives?

  • Wait until the “Estimated Delivery” window passes; if the package still hasn’t arrived, start a dispute.
  • Upload screenshots of the tracking page and provide details about your order.
  • Follow AliExpress’ instructions for evidence and keep copies of your messages with the seller.

In many cases, AliExpress sides with the buyer if the seller can’t prove delivery within the expected timeframe. For items that are damaged or significantly different from the description, you can also open a dispute and request a refund or replacement. This process is designed to protect you while giving sellers a chance to explain or resolve the issue. [jingsourcing](https://jingsourcing.com/b-how-to-buy-from-aliexpress/)

Choosing the right shipping for your needs

If you’re buying a gift for a birthday or holiday, estimate the delivery time and add a buffer of several days in case of delays. For example, if a seller promises 15–25 days, assume it could stretch to 30 or more, especially during peak seasons. In those cases, choosing faster shipping is often worth the extra cost.
